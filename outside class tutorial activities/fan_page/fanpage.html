Postmodern Jukebox Fan Site!

Postmodern Jukebox is a rotating musical collective founded by arranger and pianist Scott Bradlee.
The group is best known for covering modern day songs in different vintage genres, such as Swing and Jazz.

A Quick Bio on PMJ:

Starting in 2011 in Scott Bradlee's basement, PMJ began recording and posting Music video covers on You Tube, featuring various singers and instrumentalists, a cast that now includes well over a 70 different artists. PMJ primarily sticks to early 20th century styles, but they have even done a 60's style cover of Christina Aguilera's Genie in a Bottle.

With their virst viral video a cover of Macklemore & Ryan Lewis' "Thrift Shop" (2012) Reaching a million views in its first week, and four million views in its first year, PMJ has grown into a massive music production. They have now also toured world-wide, hitting 6 different continents with different casts performing throughout.
